Automated Proctoring

The exam proctoring process is managed through Examity® for all online courses delivered through World Campus. Examity® is a company that specializes in proctoring exams for many institutions of higher education. (For all University Park web-based course sections that have proctored exams, the management of the proctoring of exams is the responsibility of the instructor.)

Below are resources guides for getting started with Examity Automated Proctoring for instructors and user guides for students.

Considerations from Faculty

Examity has a learning curve. Using it the first time for the most important exam will cause stress among students. Many students will fail to run the examity software either because they don't meet the technical requirement or don't read/follow instructions. Failure to launch Examity happens more often with the automated option because there is no one to help out. Examity has tech support but I can't imagine how they will do this with 1000 + students taking the exam in same day. I outline other concerns in the PDF below.

I have used live proctoring (a person watches them) for my World Campus courses. What is being offered to us at this time is an automated option. More info is below. I would just caution that this is one more layer of complexity to an already complex situation. There are also several system requirements noted below. Jackie and I are going to experiment with quizzing via Zoom, a platform all our students are familiar with since we hold our live class sessions there. We have surveyed our students as well to know what technology access they have.

Examity System Requirements

  • Desktop computer or laptop (not a tablet)
  • Working built-in or external webcam and microphone
  • Internet speed must be at least 2 Mbps download and 2 Mbps upload. Determine Internet speed by running a test at: speedtest.net
