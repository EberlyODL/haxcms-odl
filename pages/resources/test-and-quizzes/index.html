Our college encourages faculty to consider a mixed approach when building assessments and considering different strategies when requiring students to demonstrate various levels of understanding and knowledge. There are a number of things to consider when creating assessments and considering academic integrity. We are finding faculty who are having the most success are those who are using a multi-pronged approach.

Considerations for a Multi-Pronged Approach

  1. Conduct a student needs assessment. What kind of technology do students have access to? What is their internet service like? Do they have things that may interfere with their test-taking abilities? Here is a sample survey that has been used by some faculty to figure out in advance what challenges may be faced during an assessment.
    1. Example: Student Needs Assessment
  2. Turn on Canvas Quiz Log
    1. Canvas Quiz log is a tool that can be turned on for each of your Canvas that will help you identify if students are navigating away from the Canvas space during quizzing.
  3. As part of your exam or quiz, ask your students to review and complete an Academic Integrity Quiz or agreement
    1. Example: Academic Integrity Agreement/quiz
  4. Use Zoom Rooms
  5. Mix the types of Questions
    1. Some faculty start out with retention questions that are multiple choice and then add a few questions later that test transfer through short response or essay.
    2. Leverage Canvas Quiz functionality
      1. What is a Quiz?
      2. Intro to Assessment Tools and Practices
  6. Reconsider your types of assessments
    1. Implement many low-stakes assessments as opposed to few high stakes assessments
    2. Have students generate material, such as a paper, project, or even a video
    3. Have students work in groups
    4. Have students submit hand-written assignments (so you can check for consistency across assignments)
    5. Have students submit e-portfolios
