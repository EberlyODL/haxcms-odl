Online Labs

There are numerous ways to approach online labs, and when faculty ask how they should go about it, the answer is always "It depends." We have created a toolkit for faculty to think about how they might go about putting a lab online. In case we shut down, faculty should be asking what is it that my students need to learn now? What can be pushed back to another time? What is the minimum viable actions students can be taking/performing. While it may not be optimal to watch a video or do a simulation, it may be the only thing we have right now. Check out our Online Lab Toolkit.

Demonstrations

Demonstrations are a great way to show students about processes or concepts. You can ask them to write something or answer questions that may require higher order thinking.

Worked Problems

One of the most foundational and important strategies for teaching Science is worked problems. It's easy in the classroom--you walk up to a chalkboard or a whiteboard, turn on an overhead procector and write on a clear plastic sheet, share problems in a powerpoint, write on an iPad and share via Doceri, or use a document camera to projector to broadcast onto a screen. How do you do this online?

Just like in the classroom, you have several options for online:

  • Use a whiteboard in Zoom to record the worked problem
  • Use your an iPad app, like Doceri or Zoom, to record your work on the iPad
  • Use a document camera to capture your work on paper to record to your computer and share with students
  • Film yourself using our lightboard in our OneButton Studio

Generative Learning

Research shows that students who are prompted to generate an answer or do something with content retain knowledge better and show increased transfer. H5P is an open-source tool we use to facilitate generative learning.
