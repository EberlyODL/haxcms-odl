Get Started

It may seem overwhelming when you think about moving your course online quickly, but there are some steps that you can take that make it a little more manageable.

  • Put your syllabus online. You can upload your syllabus as a Word Document or a PDF to Canvas so that students have access to all the important and relevant information about the course.
    • How to upload files to Canvas
  • Make a statement in your syllabus about what would happen if classes were cancelled, including how the class may change and how you will stay in contact with them.
    • Penn State Virus Info
  • If something does happen, get details about the event. Find out what Penn State is saying and the policies around the event.
  • Talk with your department. Departments may have unique strategies and plans for their own particular courses.
  • Communicate with students early and often. Use Canvas Inbox to mail students about changes that are coming and any details they need to know.
  • Make sure you set realistic goals for instruction. What can you move online and how do you ensure students will have structure and be accountable for their learning.
  • Determine course priorities. What do students really need to know?
  • Review your syllabus for places where instruction may change. How will you modify due dates and assignments? You will need to update your syllabus accordingly.
  • Realize that this is as tough on your students as it is for you. What will participation look like now and how you can be equitable and accommodating to all?
  • Make sure you continue to communicate with students and update them on details and changes they will need to know.

For more information, please contact our office.

Upcoming Training

Canvas, Kaltura, and Zoom: Enabling Continuity of Instruction

Snow day? Feeling sick? Unable to attend class? Regardless of the reason, it is important to have a plan for recovering lost class time. With Canvas, Kaltura and Zoom, you can keep students engaged and accountable for missed material. In this session, you will learn to identify content and choose the most effective Canvas delivery methods, produce reusable lectures in Zoom or Kaltura, and leverage Kaltura to increase interactivity with automatic chapters and slides, picture-in-picture, interactive transcripts, and quizzes, and evaluate student interaction using video analytics.

Objectives

Participants will be able to:

  • Identify content and choose effective delivery methods
  • Deliver content in Canvas according to best practices
  • Produce interactive videos with chapters and slides, picture-in-picture, interactive transcripts, and quizzes
  • Evaluate interaction and effectiveness using video analytics
