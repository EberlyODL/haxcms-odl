When publishing content online, it is important to ensure all accessibility standards are met. Videos require accurate captions that are used by students with disabilities as well as many students looking to increase comprehension. Visual content such as graphics and photographs require you to write meaningful alternative text and/or long descriptions. If you were to give a description of your visual content over the phone, the person on the other end should be able to visualize your content by only hearing your text description. 

When using Kaltura to upload or record Kaltura Capture videos, you have the option to create or order captions to upload your own caption file, or order free machine-generated captions and edit the captions for accuracy (machine captions are only 70-80% accurate and not 100% accessible) within the Kaltura Caption Editor. If you choose to use a YouTube video that contains machine captions that you don’t have access to edit, you can link the Youtube video into a Kaltura instance, generate machine captions and then edit them for accuracy/upload them using the same caption editing method listed above.

For more information about whether your content is accessible or not, you can review the policies and instructions on Penn State’s Accessibility Website, or contact our office for more help at odl@science.psu.edu.

For assistance with accessibility please fill out the Accessibility Consultation Form.
