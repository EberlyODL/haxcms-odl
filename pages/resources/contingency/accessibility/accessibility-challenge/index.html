In 1990, Congress passed a series of landmark anti-discrimination laws that require public and private organizations to take strides to accommodate those with recognized disabilities trying to use their services or goods. The ADA is most notorious for its rules about accessibility, but it also governs closed captioning. If a video, PowerPoint presentation, or commercial is available publicly, it is legally required to be captioned. Penn State was once sued for inaccessible content: Settlement Between Penn State University and National Federation of the Blind (psu.edu).

Therefore, our goal is to help familiarize you with some of the processes where you can make your materials more accessible to all students in accordance with Federal law. Each week, we will provide you with the challenge and instructions on how to complete the challenge. You--if you choose to accept the mission--will work through the steps to make your content more accessible and submit it to our Submission folder. Those participants who complete all 4 challenges will be entered into a drawing for some fun College of Science/ODL swag. Ultimately, the biggest prize will be your expanded ability to help make content accessible for our students. We wish you the best of luck.
