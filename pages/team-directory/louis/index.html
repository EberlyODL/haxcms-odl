Louis Leblond is the Director of Online Education in Physics and a Lecturer of Physics in the Eberly College of Science. He is also the first Tombros Fellow for Digital Learning. The Tombros Fellow of Digital Learning is a 1-year position and is responsible for the following:

  • Exploring new technologies for teaching practices by conceiving of, consulting on, and implementing new digital projects;
  • Considering research-based practices and potential mechanisms for assessment of the impacts digital technologies on student learning;
  • Sharing digital learning technologies and ideas by engaging with faculty in the college;
  • Working on public-facing projects, in resident instruction, world campus, or outreach projects, with potential for demonstrable benefits to students and ECoS;

    • Louis is working on creating an online version of PHYS 211 Mechanics and PHYS 212 Electrogmagnetism using a new tool called the IOLab, a multifunctional measuring device for physics labs.

    Dr. Leblond's other interests include:

    Online education, with a focus on developing (and assessing the efficacy) of new innovative learning tools in online education.

    Theoretical cosmology with particular emphasis on inflation, cosmic strings, dark matter and dark energy.

    High energy physics: string theory, quantum gravity and physics beyond the standard model with particular interests in the subjects of de Sitter spacetime and time-dependent backgrounds.
