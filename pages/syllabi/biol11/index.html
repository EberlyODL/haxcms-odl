Description

You are a biological being, as are the foods that you eat. We use products from other organisms to make many of the clothes that we wear, build our homes, provide medicines, and give beauty to our lives. Therefore, developing an appreciation and understanding of the biological world is important to living in this world and becoming a responsible steward of its resources. Who hasn't watched a bird and wondered what it would be like to be able to fly? To see the world from a different perspective? This course will provide an introduction to fundamental biological topics (including cells, energy transduction, genetics, evolution, organismal structure/function, ecology) for non-majors in biology-related fields.

Objectives

By the time you complete this course, you should have learned about the following:

  • The role of natural selection, which shapes organisms in response to their environment through differential reproduction.
  • The mechanisms of inheritance, and how DNA encodes the genetic information for all living organisms.
  • The chemical basis of life, the origin of life on Earth, the structure and function of the two basic types of cells, and how cells utilize energy.
  • The fundamentals of plant biology, including plant structure, response, and reproduction.
  • The basic organization of the animal body, with a focus on the digestive and reproductive systems.
  • How organisms interact with each other and their environment, and how human activities have modified the environment for other organisms.
