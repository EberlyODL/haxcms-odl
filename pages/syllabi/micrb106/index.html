Description

This course is an introductory survey course of Microbiology. This course serves as a gateway to a whole host of different disciplines in the field of Microbiology. In addition to the basic science of living things and microbes in particular the course also includes an exploration of Metabolism, Genetics, Immunology, Epidemiology, Virology, and Biotechnology. Each of these areas are covered by other entire courses or even academic majors but we will focus on the basics of each as well as other topics related to microbes and disease.

This course will involve the study of a weekly lesson or two followed by the completion of a weekly quiz and homework assignment. We will also be using the Penn State Canvas system for course communications and grading. Continue on to read the rest of the course information including the syllabus and course policies.

Grading

The grades in this class will be based on 400 points. Each lesson we will cover this semester has a quiz that will vary in length between 15 and 30 points each. Some will be longer than others depending on the amount of material covered in a particular lesson.

However, each quiz question is worth 1 point out of the 400 total. Every question is worth exactly the same as any other question, only the length of the quiz each week will vary. Do not think in terms of percentage points here, this will be a point system based on 400 total points.

Students will work on these assignments as an individual and will be responsible for completing them on time. Late assignments will have a deduction, assignments more than a couple of days late will not be accepted. Once we reach the end of the course it will not be possible to go back and complete work that is outdated by weeks or even months. Keep up on assignments to gain maximum points.

  • Grades are based on a combination of quizzes where each question is worth one point and various homework assignments worth a handful of points. Each homework assignment has a specific number of points associated with it.
  • All students are expected to complete the weekly quizzes on time. These will be available for at least one week so budget your time accordingly. You are not able to take all your tests at one time and be done. This course is paced so you can progress through at a reasonable speed.

Any questions concerning a particular quiz or homework assignment must be resolved right away. You cannot wait till the end of the semester to correct any problems. Due to time constraints it will be impossible to review all assignments on the last day in the hope of finding one or two points to meet a grade cutoff. You should ask that all grading issues be resolved as they occur during the semester. No exams will be dropped; no extra credit will be accepted. It's impossible to evaluate dozens of book reports or shoebox dioramas.
