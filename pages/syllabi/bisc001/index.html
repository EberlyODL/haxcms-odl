Description

An exploration of how cellular structures and processes contribute to life and how life displays unity even in its diversity. Students who have passed BIOL 027, 110, or 141 may not schedule this course.

Objectives

At the end of this course, a successful student will be able to:

  1. Understand basic chemical processes that take place in living organisms, including organic compounds and the basic macromolecules.
  2. Identify cell organelles and describe their functions.
  3. Understand basic energy concepts and how enzymes and membranes function.
  4. Understand cellular respiration and photosynthesis and how they relate to each other.
  5. Understand the cell cycle, mitosis, and meiosis and how they differ.
  6. Understand basic genetics (inheritance, Mendel’s Laws, DNA structure and function, gene regulation, DNA technology).
  7. Understand basic population genetics and evolution (natural selection, genetic variation, gene pools).
  8. Understand the classification of organisms, taxonomy, and phylogenetics.
  9. Understand how the biological diversity on Earth originated and survey the major groups of organisms (prokaryotes, protists, fungi, plants, animals).
  10. Describe some of the issues in medical ethics that have arisen as a result of human tissue culture.

Attendance

You will be expected to check in every day or every other day for any announcements or updates. The amount of time spent looking over the material typically is correlated with quiz and exam grade: the more time spent studying usually translates into a better grade. Although this is an online course, you should expect to spend a similar amount of time on the material that you would for an in-person class. The general rule for studying is as follows: for every one hour of lecture, you should spend 2.5 to 3 hours studying the material. That means for a three-credit course, you should spend 7.5 to 9 hours per week studying and completing assignments if you intend to do well (“A” or “B” level).

Assignments

Weekly Quizzes

Each week a short quiz (about 15 minutes) will be made available on Canvas, covering the material from the previous week’s subject(s). Each quiz will be made available starting at 12:01 AM on Monday and will run through 11:59 PM on Wednesday. This is designed to ensure that you keep up with your reading and study. The quizzes will be in a true/false, matching, or multiple-choice type format and are intended to reinforce the main concepts in each chapter. These quizzes will only be open for a limited time, so do not go for extended periods of time (days, weeks) without having logged into the course. I typically do not re-open quizzes unless in the case of technical difficulties from my end. If you miss one or a few quizzes, this will not negatively affect your grade. If you have taken all 14 quizzes, only your 10 best scores will count towards your grade.

Book Discussions

Each week, we will have discussions on Canvas about the book The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks by Rebecca Skloot. These discussions will consist of one or more discussion questions posted by the instructor, which you will respond to and discuss. The first response will take place within three days of the initial discussion question post and will be at minimum 250 words (longer posts are welcome but will not necessarily improve your grade. Quality of post and not quantity will determine your score). After everyone has had a chance to make an initial response, you will make thoughtful comments on other students’ posts on the thread. These comments will be respectful (no name calling, threats, trolling, etc.) even if one does not agree with the post in question. The normal Penn State guidelines prohibiting harassment and hate speech will be in place and will be enforced. We can discuss controversial topics like adults because we are adults.

Midterm Exam

Approximately every 4 weeks, you will have a midterm exam over the material covered. These exams will include true/false, multiple-choice, and/or matching questions; in addition there may be diagrams to label and/or short free-response questions. Exams will cover material for each “unit”: Exam 1 will cover material from the beginning of the course until the first exam, Exam 2 will cover material after Exam 1 until the second exam, and so forth. These will be delivered online, but they will be proctored using Examiny software. You will have a window of about 72 hours to take your exam, but you must schedule it in advance through the Examiny tab in Canvas. If you make changes to your scheduled exam time less than 24 hours before the start of the exam window, Examiny will charge you a fee of $3.

Final Exam

The final will be similar in format to the midterm exams. The only differences will be that the final will be slightly (about 50%) longer, and will be cumulative . The cumulative section will be about 33% of the final, and the remainder will be material covered after the third midterm. The final should be scheduled during Finals Week.
