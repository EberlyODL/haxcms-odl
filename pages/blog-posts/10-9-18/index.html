Do you know about Universal Design for Learning? Do you consider yourself a teacher, a learner or even BOTH? If so, UDL and it’s guidelines applies to YOU!

What is UDL?

Universal Design for Learning is a research and science-based framework that opens the door to understanding the way the human brain learns. It works to reduce barriers in education and promotes the creation of “expert learners.”

Who is UDL for?

UDL is intended to include everyone and their unique learning differences. It is meant for professional educators, curriculum developers, researchers, parents and anyone in a learning environment that would like to apply the practice of UDL.

What are the UDL Guidelines ?

The UDL guidelines are broken up into three categories that you work to provide multiple means of:

  • Engagement (the WHY of learning)
  • Representation (the WHAT of learning)
  • Action & Expression (the HOW of learning)

    • The guidelines work in three directions that apply to each category:

  • Access (to develop options for interest, perception and action)
  • Build (to develop effort, language, symbols, expression and communication)
  • Internalize (to develop self-management, comprehension and executive function)

    • What is the ultimate goal of UDL?

    Implementing UDL strategies in your learning paths is intended to allow all learners to become purposeful and motivated, resourceful and knowledgeable and strategic and goal-directed.
