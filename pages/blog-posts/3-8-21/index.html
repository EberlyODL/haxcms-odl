The university has posted their guidelines for Course Modes for Fall 2021. They are operating under the assumptions that:

  1. We will be able to be fully in person.
  2. Our rooms will operate at full capacity (without distancing restrictions).
  3. All students will be in residence.

These guidelines are meant to assist changes based on intentional pedagogical decisions, and to highlight how faculty might place those changes within the context of all the courses they offer.

This is a continually developing space and the University is working on FAQs which will be distributed as they are completed.

The Office of Digital Learning, the Center for Excellence in Science Education, and the Office of the Sr. Associate Dean for Instruction & Curricula are here to work with faculty, share and review what we've done and learned, and support planning for and delivery of courses.

We all share the goal of student learning. Additional information will be shared as it is created via College channels or KeepTeaching. If any faculty need assistance with planning for fall, they should contact any of our offices.
