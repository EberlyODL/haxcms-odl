Recently (May 24th - 25th), Michael Potter and I had the opportunity to present at the Tech Pros Conference at Penn State Dickinson Law. For those unfamiliar, Tech Pros is a conference that visits a different Commonwealth Campus each year to increase development and networking opportunities for IT professionals at the university. In past years, the conference has mainly focused on classroom technology, media integration, and technical support strategies. This year, the conference introduced a new track for the web, so we jumped on the opportunity to demonstrate some of the work we have been doing in Virtual Reality (VR).

As some of you might remember, the Office of Digital Learning has been working with the Forensics team to develop an interactive crime scene prototype (https://odl.science.psu.edu/blog/post/3dvr-prototype). Specifically, weâ€™ve been using an open source web platform called A-Frame to deliver images overlaid with clickable hotspots. Once clicked, the hotspots reveal dialog windows that expose users to 3D models and other significant information. Overall, we had a great time. The Tech Pros conference was rich with energy and excitement over emerging technologies, and everyone seemed eager to collaborate and share their ideas with each other. In addition to presenting, we had the opportunity to sit-in on some great panel-discussions as well as the chance to network with a number of colleagues within the university. We thank the Tech Pros organizers for having us and we look forward to sharing more advancements in web technology in the future.
